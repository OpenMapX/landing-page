The OpenMapX software is free and open source under the licenses described in our [licensing overview](https://github.com/OpenMapX/openmapx/blob/main/LICENSING.md). **The OpenMapX _name_ and _logo_ are not.** They are trademarks of {legalConfig.name}, and the copyright licenses that cover the code do **not** grant any right to use the OpenMapX name or logo.

This separation is deliberate, and common among open-source projects (Mastodon, Ghost, WordPress, Grafana, and others do the same). The open license guarantees your freedom to use, study, modify, and share the *software*. The trademark does something the code license can't: because the code and everything in it are open, the name and logo are the one reliable signal of what is genuinely **OpenMapX** — official, and what people can trust. This policy keeps that signal meaningful.

You can fork the code; you just can't present your fork as "OpenMapX."

### 1. Marks this policy covers

The word mark **"OpenMapX"** and the **OpenMapX logo**, in any form, including confusingly similar names and stylizations.

### 2. What you may do without asking

- **Run OpenMapX.** Operate your own instance — modified or unmodified — and tell people it runs OpenMapX.
- **Refer to OpenMapX truthfully.** Use the name in factual, referential ways: "compatible with OpenMapX," "an integration for OpenMapX," "built on OpenMapX," or "derived from the OpenMapX source code."
- **Keep the marks as shipped.** Leave the OpenMapX name and logo where they already appear in the unmodified software and its documentation.
- **Write and talk about OpenMapX** in articles, tutorials, talks, and community content, and run user groups or meetups about it.

### 3. What requires our written permission

- Using "OpenMapX" (or a confusingly similar name) as, or as part of, the name of **your own product, company, service, app, domain name, or social-media handle**.
- Branding a **modified or forked** version of the software as "OpenMapX," or in a way that suggests it is the official project.
- Using the OpenMapX **logo** in a modified form, or in any way that implies endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation that doesn't exist.
- Selling **merchandise** bearing the OpenMapX name or logo.

If you run a commercial service based on OpenMapX, you may state that fact truthfully ("powered by OpenMapX"), but you may not name or brand the service as OpenMapX without permission.

### 4. Using the name correctly

When you refer to OpenMapX, use it as an adjective followed by a generic noun — "the OpenMapX project," "an OpenMapX instance," "an OpenMapX integration" — rather than as your own product or company name, and don't alter the spelling or capitalization.

### 6. Reporting misuse

If you see the OpenMapX name or logo used in a way that's confusing or implies an affiliation that doesn't exist, let us know at the address above.
